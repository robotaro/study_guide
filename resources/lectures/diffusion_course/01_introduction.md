### Prior for Images
- P(x), the density function, for image processing
- The total space, filled by  20x20 grayscale patches, for example, is almost empty. 
	- These patches from images would be 400 dimensional vectors that aggregate in manifolds/hyperplanes inside these high-dimensional space. 
	- These correspond to the density function of all possible image patches 
- [IMPORTANT] The fact that we have low dimensional structure within high dimensional space is the reason why we have signal processing, image processing and others. Without this property there would be no processing  or machine learning. Nothing could have been done! It's true for text, audio, images, biological images, anything!
- The P(x) is the low-dimensional distribution inside the high dimensional space that represents the probability of that image, given its density function.
- Creating a noisy image is far more likely to appear in the empty space, than on another position inside the manifold of valid images
